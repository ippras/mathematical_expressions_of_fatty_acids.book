# Cetane number

$$CN = -0.1209 \times DU + 65.0958$$

## Abbreviations

- CN

## Description

Represents the cetane number (CN), which measures the ability of a fuel to ignite quickly after being injected into the combustion chamber. A higher value indicates better ignition quality of the fuel. This is one of the most important parameters considered during the selection of methyl esters for use as biodiesel. An adequate CN is required for good engine performance, helps ensure good cold start properties, and minimizes the formation of white smoke.
