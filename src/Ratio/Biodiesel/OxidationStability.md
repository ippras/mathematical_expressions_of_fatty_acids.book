# Oxidation stability

$$OS = -0.0384 \times DU + 7.770$$

## Abbreviations

- OS

## Description

Represents the oxidation stability of biodiesel, which is a critical parameter indicating the fuel's resistance to degradation upon exposure to oxygen over time. A higher oxidation stability ensures a longer shelf life and prevents the formation of gums, acids, and polymeric deposits that can clog fuel filters and damage engine components. It is inversely related to the degree of unsaturation (DU), as polyunsaturated fatty acids are highly susceptible to oxidation.

**Biodiesel standards**:

| Biodiesel standard         | Limit    |
| -------------------------- | -------- |
| China (GB/T 20828-2007)    | [-;+∞]   |
| Europe (EN 14214-2005)     | [6.0;+∞] |
| Russia (ГОСТ Р 53605-2009) | [6.0;+∞] |
| USA (ASTM D6751-2003)      | [3.0;+∞] |
